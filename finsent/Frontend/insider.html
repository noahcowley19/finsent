Insider Purchases (Bullish Signal)

When executives buy shares with their own money, it often indicates confidence in the company's future. Cluster buying—multiple insiders buying around the same time—is considered particularly significant.

Insider Sales (Context Required)

Sales are common and often occur for personal reasons: diversification, tax planning, home purchases, or scheduled trading plans (10b5-1). Concentrated or unusual selling may warrant attention, but sales alone don't indicate bearishness.

Cluster Transactions

When multiple insiders buy or sell within a short timeframe, it carries more weight than individual transactions. This coordinated activity may reflect shared information about company prospects.

Institutional Ownership

High institutional ownership (60%+) typically indicates a well-followed stock. Changes in institutional positions can signal shifting sentiment among professional investors.

Insider trading data comes from SEC Form 4 filings, which must be filed within 2 business days of a transaction. This data should supplement, not replace, fundamental analysis.
